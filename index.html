Spider-Man, created by Stan Lee and Steve Ditko, is a beloved superhero from Marvel Comics, known for his incredible powers and relatable struggles. Bitten by a radioactive spider, Peter Parker gains superhuman abilities like wall-crawling, web-slinging, enhanced strength, agility, and a precognitive "spider-sense". Balancing his crime-fighting duties as Spider-Man with his personal life, Peter Parker grapples with the responsibility that comes with his powers, especially after the tragic loss of his Uncle Ben. Spider-Man's popularity stems from his relatability as a young, everyday person thrust into extraordinary circumstances. His struggles with balancing his personal life, including school, work, and relationships, with his superhero duties resonate with audiences. Furthermore, the core theme of "with great power comes great responsibility," instilled in him by Uncle Ben, serves as a powerful moral compass for both the character and the audience.
